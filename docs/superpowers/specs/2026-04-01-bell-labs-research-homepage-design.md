# R.A.I.N. Lab Homepage Repositioning Design

Date: 2026-04-01

## Goal

Reposition the homepage around a sharper product promise:

- Start with a raw research question
- Show a live expert debate
- Ground strong claims in papers or explicit evidence
- Deliver angles the user would have missed, plus clear next moves

The homepage should feel broad enough to attract independent researchers, academic researchers, and R&D teams, while still being concrete enough to avoid reading like a generic AI thinking tool.

## Core Product Positioning

Primary category:

- Expert panel in a box

Secondary trust layer:

- Private by default
- Strong claims tied to papers or explicit evidence

Primary user feeling:

- "I found angles I would have missed."

Secondary user feeling:

- "I know what to do next."

## Target Audience

Homepage lead audience:

- Independent researchers

Also relevant to:

- Academic researchers
- R&D teams
- Skunkworks and advanced strategy teams exploring hard technical questions

Important distinction:

- The homepage remains broad
- The likely strongest early painful wedge remains interdisciplinary researchers who need outside critique before wasting a submission, grant, or research cycle

## Recommended Homepage Strategy

Recommended approach:

- Lead with a serious, scientific product voice
- Use an input-first hero
- Center the product around a live expert debate on the visitor's own question
- Make evidence-grounding explicit
- Show synthesis as the payoff, not the first visual

Rejected alternatives:

1. Private research companion
   - Too soft and crowded
   - Does not differentiate from generic AI assistant positioning

2. Research brief first
   - Safer, but weaker as a homepage hook
   - Undersells the multi-perspective and debate advantage already present in the product

3. Speculative debate first, citations later
   - More theatrical, but weakens trust
   - Conflicts with the desired scientific tone

## Homepage Narrative

The page should communicate this sequence:

1. The visitor brings one hard research question
2. A room of experts forms around it
3. The experts debate with evidence
4. The user leaves with the strongest explanations, open disagreements, and concrete next steps

The page should feel like a product experience, not a manifesto and not a dashboard.

## Homepage Structure

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

- Let the visitor immediately understand the product
- Make the first action obvious
- Signal scientific seriousness and evidence discipline

Recommended copy:

- Headline: `Ask a research question. Get a room full of experts.`
- Subhead: `R.A.I.N. Lab turns one raw question into a live, evidence-grounded debate across multiple expert perspectives, then distills the strongest explanations, disagreements, and next moves.`
- Primary CTA: `Start the debate`
- Trust line: `Private by default. Strong claims tied to papers or explicit evidence.`

Hero UI requirements:

- Input field for the user's own question
- Example prompt below the field
- Fast explanation of what happens after submission

### 2. Value Strip

Purpose:

- Reinforce the three main benefits in one scan

Recommended items:

- `Different perspectives, not one flat answer`
- `Claims grounded in papers`
- `Synthesis you can act on`

### 3. How It Works

Purpose:

- Explain the mechanism simply, without technical jargon

Recommended steps:

1. `You ask a hard question`
2. `A panel of experts forms around it`
3. `They debate, cite evidence, and challenge each other`
4. `You get the strongest explanations, open disagreements, and what to test next`

### 4. Live Debate Preview

Purpose:

- Show the product's differentiation in action

Requirements:

- Present a transcript-style interface, not a static summary card
- Each expert has a distinct lens
- Evidence appears inline or in expandable citations
- The visible emphasis is disagreement, pressure, and synthesis

This section must make the visitor think:

- "This is not giving me one answer."
- "This is surfacing the real argument."

### 5. Why This Exists

Purpose:

- Clarify the gap in the market

Recommended copy direction:

- `Search tools help you find papers.`
- `R.A.I.N. Lab helps you think with a room full of experts.`

This section should avoid attacking search tools. The point is not that search is bad. The point is that search alone is incomplete.

### 6. Use Cases

Purpose:

- Show breadth without losing clarity

Recommended audience framing:

- Independent researchers exploring a hard question
- Academic researchers pressure-testing a hypothesis
- R&D teams mapping competing explanations before building

### 7. Final CTA

Purpose:

- Repeat the core action after the value has been shown

Recommended CTA language:

- `Bring one hard question`

## First Session Product Contract

The homepage promise should match the first-run experience.

For a raw research question, the first session should produce:

1. A live multi-expert debate
2. Evidence-backed claims where possible
3. Explicit marking of uncertainty where evidence is weak
4. A final synthesis that includes:
   - strongest explanations
   - unresolved disagreements
   - likely objections
   - next experiments, readings, or investigation steps

The output should feel closer to a serious interdisciplinary panel than to a generic chatbot transcript.

## Tone and Brand Direction

Primary tone:

- Serious
- Scientific
- Credible

Secondary tone:

- Curious
- Cross-disciplinary
- Exploratory

Avoid:

- Whimsical research-assistant language
- Generic AI companion framing
- Overly broad "thinking partner" copy
- Vague claims about insight without showing the debate mechanism

## Success Criteria

The repositioned homepage succeeds if a visitor immediately understands:

1. This starts from my own research question
2. The system gives me multiple grounded perspectives, not one answer
3. The debate is evidence-aware, not free-form speculation
4. The product is private by default
5. The result is a useful synthesis I can act on

## Out of Scope

This spec does not define:

- visual styling details
- component-level implementation
- final frontend architecture
- launch pricing or packaging
- deeper workflow changes beyond what the homepage promises

Those belong in the implementation plan and design execution work.
